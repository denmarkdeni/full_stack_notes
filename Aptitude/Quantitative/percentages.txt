A student scored 72 marks out of 90. 
What percentage did he score? 
Later the maximum marks were increased to 100. 
What would be his percentage if marks remain the same?

    A) 80% and 72%
    B) 80% and 75%
    C) 75% and 72%
    D) 80% and 70%

🧩 Answers with Explanations:

    A) 168.75

20% = 45 → 100% = 45 × 5 = 225 → 75% = 225 × 0.75 = 168.75

    B) $1000

After 20% discount, price = 80% of original.
80% of original = 800 → Original = 800 ÷ 0.8 = 1000

    A) 4,200

A got 65% → B got 35% → 35% of 12,000 = 0.35 × 12000 = 4200

    A) 120

125% of original = 150 → Original = 150 ÷ 1.25 = 120

    B) $52.17

Selling price = Cost price × 1.15
60 = CP × 1.15 → CP = 60 ÷ 1.15 ≈ 52.17

    C) 1% decrease

Let original = 100
After 10% increase → 110
After 10% decrease → 110 × 0.9 = 99
Net decrease = 1%

    B) 40

Boys = 60% of total, girls = 40%
60% = 24 → Total = 24 ÷ 0.6 = 40

    A) 20%

Ratio 4:1 → Total parts = 5 → Water = 1/5 = 20%

    A) 2 : 1

0.15x = 0.30y → x/y = 30/15 = 2/1 → 2:1

    A) 80% and 72%

First: 72/90 × 100 = 80%
Second: 72/100 × 100 = 72%

1.Answer & Trick: A) 357
15% = 63 → 5% = 21 → 1% = 21 ÷ 5 = 4.2 → 100% = 420 → 85% = 420 × 0.85 = 357

2.Answer & Trick: B) ₹800
After 25% discount → 75% of MP = 600
So 75% = 600 → divide by 3 → 25% = 200 → 100% = 800

3.Answer & Trick: A) 400
Work backwards:
Final = 408 → after 15% decrease means it was 408 ÷ 0.85
408 ÷ 0.85 = 480 (after increase)
480 was after 20% increase → original = 480 ÷ 1.2 = 400

4.Answer & Trick: B) 9
80% passed → 20% failed
80% = 36 → 20% = 9 students failed in Maths

5.Answer & Trick: A) ₹44,800
Now he spends only 75% of new salary and saves 25%.
Savings = 25% of new salary = 11,200
25% = 11,200 → 100% = 11,200 × 4 = ₹44,800
