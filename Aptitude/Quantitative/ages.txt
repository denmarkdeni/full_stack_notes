1. The present age of a father is three times the age of his son. After 10 years, the father’s age will be twice the son’s age. Find their present ages.		

2. The ratio of the present ages of A and B is 4 : 5. Five years ago the ratio was 3 : 4. Find their present ages.

3. The present age of a mother is four times the age of her daughter. After 8 years, the mother will be three times as old as the daughter. Find their present ages.

4. The sum of the present ages of a father and son is 50 years. Five years ago, the father was four times as old as the son. Find their present ages.

5. The present age of A is twice the age of B. Ten years ago, A was three times as old as B. Find their present ages.

6. The average age of a family of four people is 25 years. After 5 years, what will be the average age of the family?

7. The present age of a man is 40 years and the present age of his son is 10 years. After how many years will the father be twice as old as his son?

8. The sum of the ages of A and B is 40 years. The difference between their ages is 8 years. Find their present ages.

9. The ratio of the present ages of two brothers is 3 : 5. After 6 years, the ratio becomes 4 : 6. Find their present ages.

10. The present age of a father is 45 years. His son is 15 years old. After how many years will the father's age be three times the son's age?

Answers : 

1. Father = 30 years, Son = 10 years

2. A = 20 years, B = 25 years

3. Mother = 64 years, Daughter = 16 years

4. Father = 37 years, Son = 13 years

5. A = 40 years, B = 20 years

6. 30 years

7. 20 years

8. A = 24 years, B = 16 years

9. No valid solution (ratio condition inconsistent)

10. 0 year
